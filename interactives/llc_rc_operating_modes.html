LLC resonant converter operating modes — ZVS operation in Regions 1 and 2

DC gain characteristic

Why two ZVS regions?

Region 1 (fsw > fr)inductive, ZVS
Region 2 (fsw < fr)inductive, ZVS
Region 3 (very low fsw)capacitive — ZCS, avoid
The LLC tank is inductive at any frequency above the Lm+Lr series resonance with Cr. This means the switch current lags the voltage, enabling ZVS turn-on for both regions 1 and 2.

Region 1 — fsw > fr

Resonating elementsLr + Cr only (like SRC)
Lm behaviorClamped by Vo, acts as passive load
ZVS mechanismMagnetizing current ensures ZVS at any load
No-load behavior✓ Stable (unlike pure SRC)
In region 1, Lm is always clamped to Vo/n by the secondary diodes, so it never participates in resonance. Only Lr and Cr resonate. The magnetizing current (ILm) creates the inductive phase lag that guarantees ZVS — even at zero load, since ILm is load-independent.

Mode 1 — t₀ to t₁  ZVS turn-on for Q1

TriggerQ2 turns OFF at t₀
ILr at t₀Negative → flows through Q1 body diode
Secondary sideD1 conducts, Io rising
LmClamped at Vo/n, linearly charging
ResonatingLr + Cr (Lm clamped out)
ILr is negative at t₀ so it flows through Q1's body diode, holding VDS=0 on Q1. Gate signal applied during this window = true ZVS. This is the key: you turn on the switch while its body diode is already conducting — no voltage across it.

Mode 2 — t₁ to t₂  Lr–Cr resonance, SRC-like

TriggerILr crosses zero (becomes positive)
Q1 stateMOSFET channel conducting (from Mode 1 gate signal)
SecondaryD1 conducting, output receiving energy
LmStill clamped, linearly charging
End conditionILr = ILm → D1 output current → 0
This mode is SRC-like: Lr and Cr form the resonant tank, Lm is frozen by the secondary voltage clamp. Energy transfers to the load. The mode ends naturally when ILr equals ILm — the diode current goes to zero with no reverse recovery.

Mode 3 — t₂ to t₃  Lm joins resonance

TriggerILr = ILm, both diodes reverse biased
SecondaryDisconnected (Vxfmr < Vo)
Lm nowFree to resonate with Lr and Cr
New resonant freqf = 1/[2π√((Lm+Lr)Cr)] — much lower
Waveform effect"Flat" plateau in ILr — actually slow resonance
EndQ1 turns OFF; low turn-off current
When both diodes block, the transformer secondary is open. Lm is no longer clamped — it joins Lr and Cr in a new, slower resonance. The waveform shows a near-flat region (it's actually a very slow sinusoid). Q1 turns off here with low current → low turn-off loss. This mode also prepares the negative ILr needed for ZVS of Q2 in the next half-cycle.
